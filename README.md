# 📊 HR Analytics — Employee Attrition Analysis & Retention Intelligence

> A two-stage data analytics project examining workforce attrition patterns 
> at NextGen Systems, delivering executive-level insights and actionable 
> retention strategies through interactive Power BI dashboards.

---

## 🔍 Problem Statement

Over the past year, NextGen Systems experienced a noticeable rise in employee 
departures, leading to declining operational efficiency, manpower shortages, 
and increased recruitment costs. Leadership needed data-backed answers to 
understand *why* employees were leaving and *which* groups were most at risk.

**Key business questions:**
- Why are employees leaving?
- Which employee segments are most likely to resign?
- Are salary, job satisfaction, or work-life balance influencing attrition?
- How can attrition be reduced before it becomes a bigger organizational risk?

---

## 🧭 Approach

The analysis was structured in two stages:

| Stage | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| **Executive Overview** | Overall attrition trends, department impact, job roles, overtime patterns |
| **Workforce Deep Dive** | Age, experience, income, satisfaction segments + competitor salary benchmarking |

---

## 📈 Key Findings

### Executive Overview
- **Attrition Rate:** 16.1% (237 out of 1,470 employees)
- **Highest attrition departments:** R&D (133 exits) and Sales (92 exits)
- **Most affected roles:** Laboratory Technicians (62), Sales Executives (57), Research Scientists (47)
- **Overtime impact:** Employees working overtime showed significantly higher attrition
- **Gender:** Attrition balanced across gender — role and workload are stronger predictors than demographics

### Workforce Deep Dive
- **Tenure pattern:** Attrition is front-loaded — freshers and early-career employees exit at the highest rates; senior workforce is highly stable
- **Income distribution:** 47.68% of exits come from the low income band; only 4.64% from very high income
- **Satisfaction signal:** Lower job satisfaction ratings (1–2) correlate with attrition rates of 22–23%, vs 11% at rating 4
- **Core insight:** Pay is market-competitive overall — the issue is **effort-reward misalignment**, not salary alone

---

## 💡 Retention Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following interventions were recommended:

1. **Strengthen early-tenure onboarding** — attrition is highest in the first 1–2 years
2. **Clarify fresher career paths** — lack of growth visibility drives early exits
3. **Boost manager-led engagement** — engagement deficits strongly predict resignations
4. **Improve early pay progression** — not just market rate, but perceived fairness of reward
5. **Balance overtime workload** — overtime is a significant burnout and attrition signal
6. **Target high-risk entry roles** — Lab Technicians and Sales Executives need dedicated retention programs
7. **Track attrition continuously** — attrition is patterned and manageable with ongoing monitoring
